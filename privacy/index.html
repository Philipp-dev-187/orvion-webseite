  • Input Monitoring: may be needed for advanced input remapping, global shortcut detection, mouse-related remapping, or similar desktop-level interactions.
  • Camera: may be needed for the camera mirror feature or any live preview integrated into the notch overlay.
  • Calendars: may be needed to display calendar events inside the overlay or other in-app widgets.
  • Reminders: may be needed to show reminders or related productivity widgets.
  • Automation / Apple Events: may be needed when Orvion interacts with supported apps or browsers, such as Safari-related automation or media control workflows.

Depending on implementation, the app may also interact with third-party applications such as Spotify, Apple Music, Safari, and supported browsers to show playback status, control media, or trigger automations requested by the user.

The current marketing copy positions Orvion as modular. This means users should be able to enable only the parts they want, and not all permissions should be required for basic use.
